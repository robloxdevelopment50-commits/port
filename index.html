Matter in My Life

Matter is anything that has mass and occupies space. Many objects and substances that I use or encounter every day are examples of matter because they have physical properties such as shape, size, color, texture, mass, and state.

📘

Notebook

State of Matter: Solid

A notebook is a solid because it has a definite shape and volume. It has physical properties such as color, thickness, size, and texture. The paper inside it is also matter because it occupies space and has mass.

🖊️

Pen

State of Matter: Solid and Liquid

A pen is a solid because it has a fixed shape and volume. It has physical properties such as length, color, weight, and smooth texture. The ink inside the pen is a liquid because it flows and takes the shape of its container.

💨

Air I Breathe

State of Matter: Gas

Air is a gas because it does not have a fixed shape or volume. It spreads out and fills the space around it. Even though we cannot see air, it is still matter because it has mass and occupies space.

These examples show that matter exists in different states: solid, liquid, and gas. They also prove that the objects and substances I use or encounter every day can be described through their physical properties.

General Chemistry Connection

Measurement

Measurement helps describe matter accurately using numbers and units. It makes scientific observations more reliable and organized.

Atomic Theory

Atomic theory explains that matter is made of atoms. It helps us understand why objects have different properties and behaviors.

Fundamental Particles

Protons, neutrons, and electrons are important parts of atoms. They help explain the structure and behavior of matter.

Coding Connection

Coding and chemistry both use logic, patterns, and problem solving. Like programming, science requires careful analysis and clear steps.

After learning about matter, measurement, atomic theory, and fundamental particles, I realized that everything around me is made up of tiny particles that are too small to see with the naked eye. I also realized that fundamental particles, such as protons, neutrons, and electrons, are important in understanding how atoms behave.

This lesson made me appreciate science more because it explains many things that happen in everyday life. It also helped me become more curious about how matter works and how everything in the universe is formed.
